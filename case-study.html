Driving a 46.3% push notification conversion uplift for Airtel

Airtel, India's largest telecom with 300M+ subscribers, was running blanket push campaigns with declining conversion rates. I led the end-to-end engagement redesign — from diagnosis to delivery — resulting in their highest-performing quarter on MoEngage.

01   The problem

Reach was high. Relevance was not.

Airtel's marketing team was sending the same promotional push notifications to their entire subscriber base — hundreds of millions of users. While delivery rates were strong, conversion (the rate at which users acted on the notification) had been declining quarter-over-quarter.

The core issue: blanket campaigns treat every user identically, regardless of their plan tier, data usage behaviour, recharge history, or geography. A prepaid user in Rajasthan and a postpaid business user in Mumbai were getting the same message at the same time.

The key question we needed to answer: Can we improve conversion without increasing send frequency, simply by sending more relevant messages to better-defined audiences?

02   Discovery

Understanding the data before touching the campaigns

Before proposing any changes, I spent the first two weeks in deep discovery — working with Airtel's CRM team to analyse existing campaign performance data and user behaviour signals available on MoEngage.

Key findings from the audit:

  • 1

    Timing misalignment

    Most pushes were sent between 10am–12pm. Engagement data showed the highest open rates for prepaid users between 7–8pm (post-work recharge window) — nearly unused by the existing schedule.

  • 2

    Irrelevant offer targeting

    High-ARPU postpaid users were receiving prepaid recharge offers. Lapsed users were getting upsell notifications. Audience–offer mismatch was the single largest driver of low conversion.

  • 3

    No lifecycle differentiation

    New users (0–30 days), active users, and churning users all received identical campaign flows. There was no onboarding journey, no retention trigger, and no win-back logic.

  • 4

    Copy was generic

    Push copy was not personalised beyond the user's first name. No dynamic content, no location-aware offers, no behavioural triggers.

03   Approach

A segmentation-first redesign of the campaign architecture

Working with Airtel's marketing and CRM teams, I proposed restructuring their entire push campaign architecture around three principles: right audience, right message, right time.

  • 1

    Audience segmentation by behaviour, not just plan type

    We created six core segments using MoEngage's segmentation engine: active prepaid, lapsing prepaid, active postpaid, new users (≤30 days), high-ARPU users, and dormant users. Each received a distinct campaign track with tailored offers.

  • 2

    Send-time optimisation

    Enabled MoEngage's intelligent send-time optimisation (STO) for prepaid segments, shifting delivery to each user's personal engagement window rather than a fixed broadcast time.

  • 3

    Dynamic personalisation in copy

    Introduced Liquid-style personalisation tokens for plan expiry dates, last recharge amount, and recommended plan tier — making each notification feel individually relevant.

  • 4

    Lifecycle journeys for new and lapsing users

    Built two new automated flows: a 4-step new user onboarding journey (days 1, 3, 7, 14) and a 3-step lapsed user win-back sequence with escalating offers.

  • 5

    A/B testing framework

    Set up systematic A/B tests across copy variants, CTA placement, and offer type — establishing a feedback loop so Airtel's team could continue optimising independently after handover.

04   Results

Conversion improved across every segment

05   Learnings

What I'd take into a PM role

  • Data before solutions. The instinct to jump to "fix the copy" was wrong. The audit revealed the root cause was audience–offer mismatch — copy was a symptom, not the problem. Discovery first, always.
  • Segmentation is a product decision. Defining the right audience segments required understanding the business model (ARPU, churn triggers, lifecycle value) — not just the tool. This is PM work wrapped in SE packaging.
  • Scalable systems beat one-time wins. The A/B testing framework and documented playbooks meant Airtel's team could continue improving independently. A handover that creates dependency isn't a handover.
  • Reduction is a result too. The 38% drop in unsubscribes was as important as the conversion lift — it signalled we were building a sustainable relationship with users, not burning through the audience.
